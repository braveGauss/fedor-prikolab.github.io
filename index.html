Research Interests

I am interested in algebraic and geometric methods in quantum information, with a focus on inverse problems (such as quantum tomography and Hamiltonian learning) and control-theoretic questions in quantum systems. My background includes projects on Stallings' folding in geometric group theory, control theory on the Heisenberg group, semisimple Lie algebras and highest-weight theory, the Yang–Baxter equation, and a reading course in ergodic theory with project in Gauss map. I now hope to use these tools to study how quantum information is encoded, compressed, and reconstructed, and to explore future connections to imaging and video compression in a quantum setting.

University of Toronto Course Projects and Reading Course Notes

  • From Vertex Models to Quantum Spin Chains: An Introductory Exposition of the Yang–Baxter Equation
    Research course project for PHY479. This exposition develops the connection between classical vertex models and quantum spin chains, introducing the Yang–Baxter equation and transfer-matrix formalism in an accessible way. [PDF]
  • Reading Course Notes on Complex Semisimple Lie Algebras
    Notes for the reading course MAT492Y1 at the University of Toronto Mississauga, focusing on highest weight theory, Verma modules, and the classification of complex semisimple Lie algebras in the spirit of Serre, Fulton–Harris, and Hall. [PDF]
  • From Queen Dido to the Heisenberg Group: Control-Theoretic Perspective
    Course project for MAT482H (Topics in Mathematics – Control Theory). This work connects the classical Queen Dido isoperimetric problem to geodesics on the Heisenberg group, highlighting a control-theoretic viewpoint on sub-Riemannian geometry. [PDF]
  • Topological Model for Free Groups and Stallings' Folding
    Course project for MAT382H (Topics in Mathematics – Geometric Group Theory), presenting a topological model for finitely generated subgroups of free groups via Stallings' folding algorithm. [PDF] As part of this project, I also created an interactive web app for testing Stallings' folding on free groups of rank 2 and 3, available at fedorstallings.com .
